History of the FIFA World Cup


Analysing Trends in Goals Scored and Average Attendance per World Cup

Visualisation of FIFA World cup statistics collected by fans and collated into an open-source kaggle dataset.
This Dataset provides a breakdown of all World Cups held in the period 1930 - 2014 and tracks stats such as goals scored and match attendance
Additionally provides a breakdown of all tournament podium finishes per edition of the World cup.

Analysing trends in Goal Scored across each edition



As per the visualisation above, it can be observed that the number of goals scored per edition of the FIFA World cup has followed an overall linear trend.
Large Spikes can be seen in editions where the number of matches has increased.
Drops in goals scored can be attributed to the changing nature and style of the game in response to new mentalities demonstrated by individual players and teams.

Average Attendance per World Cup Edition


The visualisation above seeks to visualise the average Tournament attendance per edition of the world cup.
The drops in attendance can be attributed to significantexternal events such as the World Wars

World Cup Hosts


As we can see, it appears that the majority of tournaments are hosted by European or North/South American countries.
This shows the popularity of the sport in those regions.
The majority of tournaments are also predominantly won by European countries as will be explored later.

Away goals indicate goals scored by a team playing in their Away kit while Home Goals indicate that the team is playing in their Home kit. This may affect performance as we observe below.

World Cup Goal Distribution amongst participating countries


Based off the visualisation of goals marked as away, it appears that the best performing team by far is the Brazilian National Team. However, the max magnitude of the goals only ranges up to around 45.




Overall, it seems that teams tend to score signficantly more goals when playing in their home kit.
We can observe Brazil having scored 180 home goals as opposed to 45 away goals.

Donut Chart: World Cup Winners


It is also useful to breakdown which countries are most successful in the World Cup over the years

The chart above displays that the most succesful team in the FIFA World Cup is Brazil followed by both Germany and Italy.

Other high performing teams include Argentina, Uruguay, France, England and Spain.

All World Cup Podium Places


In addition to visualling the winners, sometimes it is also nessecary to visualise how many podium places a country has had.
A podium place is defined as coming in the top 4.
It is evident that Germany has had the most podium finishes with a grand total of 12.
Brazil is a close second with 11 podium finishes, however with a larger number of tournament wins.
